verb, used with object

1. to free (grain) from the lighter particles of chaff, dirt, etc.

2. to separate or distinguish (valuable from not valuable tweets)

How it works (continued)

If you follow more than a few people on Twitter, you probably find yourself reading a lot of tweets that you don't care about. You spend time sifting through stuff that isn't funny, interesting, or relevant for you, to get to the stuff that is.

wynno helps you solve this problem. Think of it as a personalized spam filter for Twitter, where spam is any tweet you don't care about and would rather not have to spend the time reading.

wynno divides your Twitter timeline into two timelines, one with all the tweets we think you'll like (Good Stuff), and one with the stuff you won't (The Rest). But taste is unique to each person; how can wynno guess which tweets you will like?

You train it, by voting or on tweets. Voting teaches our algorithms about your preferences. The more you vote, the better wynno will get at identifying all the tweets you like, and excluding all the ones you'd have been just as happy to miss.

You can also make sure you never miss tweets from certain people, or that contain certain keywords, by adding those users/keywords to your Heard list. Heard tweets always show up in Good Stuff. Conversely, you can mute certain users or keywords--these tweets go straight to The Rest.
