Let's walk through the creation of a filter.

Filters are simply rules you make about whether to 'hear' or 'mute' different types of tweets. To make one, you use the Filter Builder, which you can open by clicking the button in the navigation bar. No need to open it now! We've reproduced the Filter Builder here, broken down into parts which we'll now explain. At the end, if you're signed in you'll be able to save the filter you've created.

Create a filter

There are three steps to making a filter:

1. How

This step defines whether the filter should 'hear' or 'mute' the tweets that satisfy its conditions. Tweets caught by a 'mute' filter will be moved to The Rest; tweets caught by a 'hear' filter will stay in the Good Stuff.

Click the dropdown button above to select one of the options. This step is required.

This step defines what users the filter applies to.

By default, a filter applies to tweets by any user. So you're not required to do anything in this step, as long as you give your filter more specific conditions in step 3.

If you want the filter to apply to tweets by particular users, just enter their Twitter username(s) in the input field and click the Add button. Usernames are not case sensitive, so no need to worry about capitalization.

As an example, you can make a filter that mutes a user entirely: just add their username here, and you're done. You can skip to the Save Filter button below—click it and your filter will be applied!

Active filters are applied to tweets in the order they are listed, that is, from the top down. That means if a tweet matches the criteria of both a hear filter and a mute filter, the filter that is listed first is the one that gets applied. Since active filters are currently listed in the order in which they were created, older filters take priority over newly created filters. This chronological priority is only temporary, though; we are hard at work on a drag-and-drop feature that will allow you to easily reorder your filters however you want.
